About Me

"Ta-Ju, you are a gifted painter, could you paint a picture of our trip to Sun Moon Lake for me?" requested by my father while he was lying in bed at the wardroom. I spent five nights recalling the beautiful scenery and the splendid moments in that family trip, in an effort to reflect the happiness into my painting. "This is a very vivid painting, it all comes back to me now," claimed by my father while holding my finished painting. I was tearful when I heard his comment. My father died a week later due to lung carcinoma. The last mile of my father’s life journey was fortunately prolonged by the doctor to three years, and it turned out a profound lesson to me. As an eight-year-old child, I stayed at my father’s bedside, suffering the pains he suffered for three years. It was also this wardroom lesson that made me a genuine “empathetic listener”.

Unfortunately, after my father’s leave, there were not many people listening to a single-parent child like me. The feeling of loss and loneliness in me thus had me turn to an emotional outlet, where I found a bit inner peace. I enrolled in the talented program in arts since the first day of my junior high school. Coincidentally, for the following three years, I had immersed myself in learning and playing around with various forms of visual arts, including sketch, watercolor, Chinese Calligraphy, sculpture, oil painting and ads design. Whenever I was drawing, painting or carving, I was so focused that I bore no more burdens. On the other hand, my artistic education had nurtured my keen sense of texture, color, size and shape, which later proved fundamental for me to discern multifarious thermoplastic material such as polyether ether ketone (PEEK) and to design the first PEEK 3D printer.

Another emotional outlet of mine was a church in the Hsinchu County in Taiwan. In this church, I came across many teenagers who had similar background to mine. We read the Bible, sang and set prayers together. Gradually, we accompanied each other, shared our stories and eventually became a family. Such company from my brothers and sisters ended up my indispensable spiritual support. In addition to my endearing companions, I was bestowed euphonious blessings from time to time. Periodically, we would volunteer to organize church camps and took care of kids whose parents might have just passed away. In the beginning, I believed I was consoling these kids. Nevertheless, while I was holding their little hands and looking at their smiley and naïve faces, I realized I was in fact the one being consoled. My experience with church and Jesus Christ was crucial to my life. It not only found me a home, but also instilled in me a spirit of caring for others and sacrifice. Such a spirit, should be easily observed in my motivation to design a 3D printer.

Although my artistic and humanistic orientations seemed prominent during my adolescence, they didn’t inhibit my propensity and aptitude for science. My potential in science can be firstly seen in my academic performance in Physics at Tamkang University, Taiwan. Throughout my college years, I had explored varied foundation knowledge of science among which were, Mechanics, Electronics, Electromagnetism, Optics and Thermodynamics. I also exploited subjects including Quantum Physics, Quantum Mechanics and even “Theory of Relativity” to push the limit of my potential. Thanks to my aptitude for science, I graduated as the 4th place out of 59 students from the Department of Physics. Other than my academic record, my capacity in science and mechanical engineering can also be observed in my success in building the first 3D printer that accommodated the unique qualities of PEEK.

“All of life is a coming home. Salesmen, secretaries, coal miners, beekeepers, sword swallowers, all of us. All the restless hearts of the world, all trying to find a way home.” 《Patch Adams》, 1998.
